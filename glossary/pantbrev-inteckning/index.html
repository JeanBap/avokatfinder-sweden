Pantbrev

Pantbrev Sweden – Mortgage Deed and Property Pledge

A pantbrev (mortgage deed) is the legal instrument by which Swedish real property is pledged as security for a loan. Unlike in many countries, the pantbrev is not created specifically for each new mortgage — it is an instrument that can be reused between different lenders and loan arrangements, remaining valid as long as the property exists.

To create a new pantbrev, the property owner applies to Lantmäteriet for an inteckning (encumbrance registration) in the fastighetsregistret. Stämpelskatt of 2% of the pantbrev amount is payable on creation. Once created, the pantbrev can be pledged (pantförskrivas) to a lender as security for a loan, and subsequently pledged to a different lender when the loan is refinanced — without incurring new stamp duty.

When a property is purchased, existing pantbrev typically transfer with the property. Buyers and their lenders can use existing pantbrev for the new mortgage, subject to the lender's satisfaction with the amounts and the registration. This reuse mechanism significantly reduces refinancing and remortgaging costs in Sweden compared to countries where mortgage instruments must be recreated.

Key Facts

Stamp duty on new pantbrev2% of pantbrev amount
ReuseYes — same pantbrev used across successive mortgages
RegistrationLantmäteriet (fastighetsregistret)
TypesElectronic or paper
ReleaseSeller's lender releases on full redemption

Pantbrev can be registered electronically (elektroniska pantbrev) or in paper form (skriftliga pantbrev). Electronic pantbrev are held in Lantmäteriet's system, while paper pantbrev are physical documents that must be physically delivered to lenders and returned when loans are repaid. Most modern transactions use electronic pantbrev.

When selling, the seller's lender releases the pantbrev after receiving the redemption funds. The buyer's lender then takes the released pantbrev (or new ones are created if additional security is needed). The estate agent or the parties' lawyers coordinate this exchange as part of the completion process.

⚠ Common MistakeCreating unnecessary new pantbrev when existing ones on the property are sufficient for the new mortgage. This triggers avoidable 2% stämpelskatt. Always check what pantbrev exist before requesting new ones.
💡 Expert TipWhen buying, ask the estate agent what pantbrev currently exist on the property (visible in the fastighetsregistret) and confirm with your lender whether they are sufficient or if new ones are needed. This determines your stamp duty exposure.

Frequently Asked Questions

What happens to pantbrev when a mortgage is fully repaid?

The pantbrev is returned to the owner (or held electronically in their name). It remains valid and can be used as security for future borrowing without new stamp duty.

Can a pantbrev be cancelled?

Yes — applying to Lantmäteriet for a dödning (cancellation). This is advisable if the pantbrev amount is no longer needed, as outstanding pantbrev can complicate future transactions.

Is there a maximum number of pantbrev on one property?

No statutory limit. Multiple pantbrev can exist simultaneously, ranked by priority order (prioritet) based on the date of registration.
