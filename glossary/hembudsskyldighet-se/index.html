Hembudsskyldighet (Pre-emption Right in Housing Cooperatives)

Hembudsskyldighet is the statutory right of a Swedish housing cooperative (bostadsrattsforening) to buy back a member's bostadsratt before it is sold to an external buyer, at the same price agreed with that buyer.

Under Swedish law, a bostadsrattsforening's stadgar (statutes) may include a hembudsskyldighet clause, giving the association (or its designated members) the right to match an agreed external sale price and purchase the outgoing member's andel instead. This is Sweden's equivalent of the Norwegian forkjopsrett.

If hembudsskyldighet applies, the seller must formally offer the bostadsratt to the association (or qualifying members) at the same price and terms agreed with the external buyer. The association typically has one month to decide whether to exercise the right. If it declines — or fails to respond within the period — the sale proceeds to the external buyer.

Not all bostadsrattsforeningar have hembudsskyldighet in their statutes. Many do not. Buyers should check the foreningen's stadgar before making an offer — if hembudsskyldighet applies, the purchase timeline extends by at least a month while the pre-emption period runs.

The right cannot be exercised to buy at a lower price than you agreed — it must match your terms exactly. If the association exercises its right, your purchase does not proceed and you should receive your deposit back under the contract's standard terms.

Hembudsskyldighet is distinct from the forhandsavtal (pre-contract) sometimes used for new-build bostadsratt — that is a different mechanism giving future members priority to purchase units in a new development before they are offered publicly.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Assuming hembudsskyldighet does not apply without checking the stadgar is a costly mistake. Always request the statutes from the seller or the foreningen before submitting an offer.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: If you are buying in a forening with hembudsskyldighet, write a contract completion date that accommodates the pre-emption period. Trying to push for a fast completion will cause stress and potential breach.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does hembudsskyldighet apply to all Swedish bostadsratt sales?

No. It only applies if the foreningen's stadgar contain the clause. Many associations have no such clause and sales proceed directly to external buyers.

Can the association buy at a lower price?

No. The right must be exercised at identical terms to those agreed with the external buyer. It is a right to buy on the same terms, not a right to renegotiate.

What happens to my deposit if hembudsskyldighet is exercised?

Standard Swedish property contracts include provisions for this scenario. The deposit is returned to you. Ensure your contract explicitly addresses this before you sign.
