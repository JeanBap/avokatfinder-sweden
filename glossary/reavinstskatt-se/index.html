Reavinstskatt (Capital Gains Tax on Property)

Reavinstskatt is Sweden's capital gains tax on property sales, levied at 22% of the net gain on freehold property and 22% on bostadsratt sales — with a partial deferral mechanism (uppskov) available when buying a new primary residence.

When selling Swedish property, any gain above the adjusted acquisition cost (omkostnadsbelopp) is subject to 22% reavinstskatt. For freehold property (fastighet), 22/30ths of the gain is taxable at the 30% capital gains rate — which effectively produces a 22% net rate. For bostadsratt sales, the same 22% effective rate applies.

The omkostnadsbelopp deductible from the sale price includes the original purchase price, documented improvement costs (forbattringsutgifter) for works lasting at least five years, and selling costs such as the estate agent's commission. Meticulous record-keeping of renovation invoices can significantly reduce the taxable gain.

Sweden's uppskov mechanism allows sellers to defer reavinstskatt when they purchase a new primary residence of equal or greater value within a specified period. The deferred gain (uppskovsbelopp) is then taxable when the replacement property is sold, or when the owner dies. An annual deferral fee (uppskovsrantan) of 0.5% of the deferred amount is levied each year the deferral is active.

Non-residents who sell Swedish property are subject to reavinstskatt on Swedish-source gains. Double taxation treaties with many countries may reduce or offset the liability — consult a specialist before selling.

The tax is reported in the seller's annual income tax return (inkomstdeklaration) for the year of sale and must be paid even before the return is filed if the amount exceeds a threshold.

Key Facts

Rate22% effective (22/30ths × 30%)
DeductiblePurchase price + improvements + selling costs
DeferralUppskov when buying replacement primary residence
Deferral fee0.5% per year of deferred amount
Non-residentsSubject to same tax; DTA may reduce liability

Common Mistake

Watch out: Improvements must have been completed within the past five years to be deductible. If you renovated more than five years ago, those costs cannot reduce your taxable gain. Plan major renovations strategically relative to your likely selling timeline.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Apply for uppskov at the time of filing your inkomstdeklaration — it is not granted automatically. Failing to claim it in the correct tax year means you pay reavinstskatt immediately with no recourse.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I defer reavinstskatt indefinitely with repeated uppskov?

Each new property purchase can carry forward the existing uppskov. In theory, deferral continues until you sell without buying a replacement — but the annual 0.5% deferral fee accumulates.

What is uppskovsrantan?

It is the annual fee charged on the deferred gain — currently 0.5% of the uppskovsbelopp per year. It is treated as income (not as a capital gains deduction) in your annual tax return.

Is the reavinstskatt rate the same for all property types?

The 22% effective rate applies to both fastighet and bostadsratt. Agricultural and commercial property may be subject to different treatment.
