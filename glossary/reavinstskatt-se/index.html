Reavinstskatt

Reavinstskatt Sweden – Capital Gains Tax on Property Sales

Sweden's reavinstskatt (capital gains tax on property) is levied at 22% on taxable gains arising from the sale of real property and bostadsrätter. It is reported on the annual income tax return (inkomstdeklaration) for the year in which the sale is completed. The effective rate may be lower than the headline 22% because of various deductions and the possibility of deferral.

The taxable gain is the sale price minus: the acquisition cost, improvement costs (förbättringsutgifter — documented capital improvements, not maintenance), and selling costs (agent commission, legal fees, styling costs). Improvements must be documented by receipts; undocumented costs are disallowed. The resulting gain is multiplied by 22/30 to give the taxable amount, on which 30% income tax is applied — producing an effective rate of 22%.

The most important relief is the uppskov (deferral): if the seller purchases a new principal residence in Sweden within a qualifying period, they can defer the capital gains tax on the rolled-over amount. The deferred amount bears annual interest (ränta på uppskov) at 0.5% of the deferred gain, which is added as income each year. The deferred gain becomes taxable when the replacement property is eventually sold (or if deferral conditions are no longer met).

Key Facts

Rate22% effective (30% × 22/30)
DeductibleAcquisition cost + improvements + selling costs
DeferralUppskov — unlimited since 2020
Uppskov interest0.5% pa on deferred amount
Loss deductibility50% of loss deductible

Since 2020, the maximum uppskov amount is unlimited (previously capped at SEK 3 million). This change significantly expanded the deferral benefit for sellers of high-value properties. The deferral does not have to be claimed at the time of sale — it can be applied in subsequent tax returns within the filing period.

Losses on property sales are deductible against other capital income in the same year or carried forward. Capital losses on real property are deductible at 50% (not 100%), so a loss of SEK 500,000 reduces taxable capital income by SEK 250,000. This partial deductibility is a common source of planning errors.

⚠ Common MistakeNot documenting improvement costs with original receipts. HMRC-style reconstruction from memory is not accepted by Skatteverket — only original receipts reduce the gain.
💡 Expert TipCreate a property cost file from day one of ownership. Keep every receipt for kitchens, bathrooms, windows, and extensions. The tax saving on a properly documented improvement project can be worth tens of thousands of kronor.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does reavinstskatt apply to bostadsrätter?

Yes. Gains on bostadsrätter are subject to the same 22% rate as gains on real property. The calculation method is slightly different (acquisition cost includes the share of the BRF's debt at purchase and sale dates).

Can I defer the gain if I am moving abroad?

Generally no. Uppskov requires purchase of a qualifying replacement principal residence in Sweden. Moving abroad typically triggers crystallisation of deferred gains.

When must reavinstskatt be paid?

It is declared on the inkomstdeklaration filed by 2 May of the year following the sale. Tax is due with the final tax settlement in the autumn. Preliminary tax may need to be paid if the gain is large.
