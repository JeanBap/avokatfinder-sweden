Energideklaration (Energy Performance Certificate)

An energideklaration is Sweden's mandatory energy performance certificate for buildings, required at the point of sale or new tenancy. It rates the property's energy consumption and is registered with Boverket.

All Swedish residential and commercial buildings sold or rented since 2009 must have a valid energideklaration registered with Boverket (the National Board of Housing, Building and Planning). The certificate rates the building's energy use in kWh per square metre per year and assigns it to an energy class from A (most efficient) to G (least efficient).

For detached houses (villor), the energideklaration must be carried out by an accredited energy expert (energiexpert). For apartment buildings, the entire building is assessed, and the result applies to all units within it. Individual bostadsratt buyers receive the building's energy class, not a unit-specific assessment.

The seller or landlord is legally responsible for ensuring a valid energideklaration exists and is made available to the buyer or tenant. Failing to provide one does not invalidate the sale but can result in administrative fines from Boverket.

Sweden's building energy regulations have tightened considerably as the EU moves toward nearly-zero-energy building (NZEB) standards. Older properties with poor energy ratings may face higher running costs and could require significant investment to meet future standards.

The energideklaration is publicly searchable via Boverket's online database using the property's address. Buyers can — and should — look this up independently before viewing, as it reveals the building's energy consumption independently of what the seller discloses.

Common Mistake

Watch out: An old energideklaration (still within its 10-year validity) may not reflect a recently renovated building's actual performance. Ask the seller for renovation records to assess whether a fresh certificate is warranted.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Search Boverket's database for the energideklaration before arranging a viewing. An E, F, or G rating on an old house means higher heating bills and potential mandatory upgrades — factor this into your offer price.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who pays for the energideklaration?

The seller (or landlord for rentals) pays for the assessment. It is not the buyer's responsibility to commission it.

Does a poor energy class affect the mortgage?

Swedish banks do not currently adjust mortgage terms based on energy class, but a poor rating affects running costs and resale value, which lenders consider indirectly.

How long is an energideklaration valid?

Ten years from the date of assessment, unless the building undergoes significant renovation that changes its energy performance, in which case a new assessment is required.
