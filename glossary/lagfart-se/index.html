Lagfart (Title Registration)

Lagfart is the official registration of property ownership in Sweden's land register (fastighetsregistret), maintained by Lantmateriet. Without lagfart, the buyer lacks full legal title and cannot mortgage the property.

After a Swedish property purchase is completed, the buyer must apply for lagfart (title registration) with Lantmateriet, the Swedish mapping, cadastral, and land registration authority. The application must be filed within three months of the purchase deed (kopekontrakt) being signed. The stamp duty (stampleskatt) on lagfart is 1.5% of the purchase price or the property's tax assessment value, whichever is higher.

Without lagfart, the buyer is not officially recorded as the property owner in the fastighetsregistret. While the purchase contract itself creates a binding obligation between buyer and seller, only lagfart creates enforceable rights against third parties — including other creditors or future buyers.

Lagfart is also required before the buyer can register any mortgage (pantbrev) against the property. Banks will not release mortgage funds until lagfart has been applied for and the pantbrev is in place.

For bostadsratt (housing cooperative) transfers in Sweden, the equivalent process is handled by the bostadsrattsforening (cooperative board), which updates its own register — no Lantmateriet registration is required because you are buying a membership right, not freehold title.

Applications for lagfart can be submitted online through the Lantmateriet portal. Processing time is typically a few weeks for straightforward cases, longer if there are queries about the purchase deed's validity.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Missing the 3-month lagfart deadline triggers penalty fees. Set a calendar reminder immediately after signing — the conveyancing lawyer typically handles this but confirm they have it in their workflow.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: If you are buying jointly, both names should appear on the lagfart application exactly as you want them on the title register. Correcting ownership proportions later requires a new transfer and another stamp duty payment.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between lagfart and pantbrev?

Lagfart registers ownership. Pantbrev creates a mortgage charge against the property. You must have lagfart before you can register a pantbrev.

Who pays the lagfart stamp duty?

By convention, the buyer pays. It is 1.5% of the purchase price for private buyers (4.25% for legal entities such as companies).

Can I sell without lagfart?

Technically yes, but it creates complications. Any buyer will require you to apply for and obtain lagfart before completion because they need clear title to register their own lagfart.
