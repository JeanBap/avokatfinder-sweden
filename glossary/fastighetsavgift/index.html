Fastighetsavgift

Fastighetsavgift Sweden – Annual Property Tax (Municipal Charge)

The fastighetsavgift (municipal property charge) is Sweden's annual tax on residential real property, replacing the former fastighetsskatt in 2008. It is charged on all one- and two-family homes and apartments held as real property (not bostadsrätter, which are charged differently via the BRF structure). The charge is capped at a statutory maximum and is significantly lower than the pre-2008 fastighetsskatt.

The fastighetsavgift is set annually and capped at the lower of: (a) 0.75% of the official assessed taxable value (taxeringsvärdet) for single-family houses, or (b) the statutory annual maximum (SEK 9,525 per year for houses in 2024, adjusted periodically). In practice, most Swedish homes pay the maximum cap rather than the percentage rate, as assessed values are typically high.

New residential construction is exempt from fastighetsavgift for the first 15 years after construction is completed. This exemption makes new-build investment properties more attractive from a holding cost perspective, though VAT applies on the purchase instead.

The fastighetsavgift is declared and paid via the Skatteverket annual tax assessment. Property owners do not need to file a separate return — the charge appears automatically on the slutskattebesked (final tax settlement). It is assessed based on ownership on 1 January of the relevant tax year.

For bostadsrätter, the BRF pays a reduced fastighetsavgift on the building (or fastighetsskatt for commercial portions), which is factored into the månadsavgift charged to members. Individual bostadsrättshavare do not pay fastighetsavgift directly.

⚠ Common MistakeConfusing the pre-2008 fastighetsskatt (a much higher rate) with the current fastighetsavgift. Some older financial analyses of Swedish property still reference fastighetsskatt rates, which can significantly overstate the tax burden.
💡 Expert TipWhen calculating the yield on a Swedish investment property, remember that the fastighetsavgift cap means tax is predictable and low for most residential properties. Focus due diligence instead on maintenance reserves, renovation needs, and rental regulation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is fastighetsavgift deductible against rental income?

Yes. It is a deductible cost against income from rental of real property (kapitalinkomst).

Does the exemption apply to purchased new-build properties?

Yes, the 15-year exemption follows the property based on when it was built, not when you bought it. A 10-year-old new-build still has 5 years of exemption remaining.

What is the taxeringsvärde?

The official tax assessment value set by Skatteverket, typically 75% of estimated market value at the most recent general assessment. It is used as the basis for fastighetsavgift but is generally lower than market price.
