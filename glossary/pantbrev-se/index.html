Pantbrev (Mortgage Deed)

A pantbrev is Sweden's mortgage instrument — a formal document registering a debt charge against a property in the fastighetsregistret. Existing pantbrev can be reused, saving the buyer stamp duty on remortgage.

In Sweden, a mortgage is created by registering a pantbrev (mortgage deed) against the property in Lantmateriet's fastighetsregistret. The pantbrev states a nominal amount (the charge value). The bank then holds the physical pantbrev (or its digital equivalent, the e-pantbrev) as security for the loan.

Creating a new pantbrev requires paying stampleskatt of 2% of the pantbrev's nominal value. However, because pantbrev are reusable instruments — they stay registered even after a loan is repaid — buyers can benefit from existing pantbrev registered by previous owners. If the seller's pantbrev covers the buyer's borrowing needs, the bank simply takes over the existing pantbrev without triggering new stamp duty.

When stamp duty savings are possible, the estate agent's handover documents will list existing pantbrev by value. Your bank will advise whether additional pantbrev are needed. This analysis should happen early in the mortgage process, not after exchange.

E-pantbrev (electronic mortgage deeds) have replaced paper documents for most new registrations since 2009. They are held in a central registry and transferred between lenders electronically, streamlining remortgaging.

If you repay your mortgage and want to cancel the pantbrev, you can apply to Lantmateriet for dodning (deregistration). However, it is usually more practical to leave them in place for future use — cancellation triggers an administrative fee with no corresponding benefit unless you are selling to a cash buyer.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Ordering unnecessary new pantbrev wastes money. Always check what pantbrev the seller has registered before your bank applies for new ones. The difference can be tens of thousands of SEK.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Ask the bank to confirm in writing how much of your borrowing they can cover using existing pantbrev. Negotiate this before you commit to the purchase price, as stamp duty on new pantbrev is a real additional cost.

Frequently Asked Questions

If I sell my property, what happens to my pantbrev?

The pantbrev remain registered but are returned to you (or cancelled) at settlement. The new buyer and their bank decide whether to use your existing pantbrev or register new ones.

What is the difference between a pantbrev and a hypotekslav?

A hypotekslav (mortgage bond) is an older instrument no longer used. Pantbrev are the current standard. Both registered charge against property value; pantbrev are the live mechanism.

Can I use one pantbrev across multiple properties?

No. Each pantbrev is registered against a specific property. If you own multiple properties and want to use them all as security, you need separate pantbrev for each.
