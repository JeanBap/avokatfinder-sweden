Bygglov

Bygglov Sweden – Building Permit Requirements and Process

A bygglov (building permit) is the municipal authorisation required before commencing most construction, extension, conversion, or external alteration work on buildings in Sweden. It is issued by the local building committee (byggnadsnämnden) and is governed by the Plan and Building Act (Plan- och bygglagen, PBL).

Smaller projects may instead use a simplified notification procedure (anmälan) rather than a full bygglov. Anmälan applies to works such as loft conversions, installation of fireplaces, or construction of smaller outbuildings (attefallsåtgärder up to prescribed dimensions). The municipality must respond within ten weeks.

The bygglov application must include architectural drawings, a site plan, and in some cases environmental assessments. The municipality assesses the application against the detailed plan (detaljplan) for the area. If no detaljplan exists, a broader assessment of compatibility with surrounding development is made.

A granted bygglov typically lasts two years for starting construction and five years for completion. Construction must begin within the start period and be completed within the total period, or a new bygglov must be applied for. The municipality can grant extensions in certain circumstances.

After completion, a startbesked (start notice) must be obtained before work begins, and a slutbesked (completion notice) before the building can be used. These procedural steps are mandatory and separate from the bygglov itself — skipping them is a common and costly mistake.

⚠ Common MistakeBeginning construction before receiving the startbesked even when bygglov has been granted. The startbesked is a separate mandatory step and construction without it risks enforcement action.
💡 Expert TipCheck the local municipality's detaljplan before purchasing land or committing to a building project. Plans are publicly available and determine what can be built by right — reviewing them before purchase avoids expensive surprises.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does a bygglov automatically allow me to start building?

No. After receiving bygglov, you must obtain a startbesked from the municipality before commencing work. This requires submitting a control plan.

Is a bygglov required for solar panels in Sweden?

Not typically for roof-mounted panels on existing houses outside protected areas. Many municipalities have streamlined procedures, but checking with the local byggnadsnämnden is always advisable.

Can I appeal a refused bygglov?

Yes. Refusals can be appealed to the County Administrative Board (länsstyrelsen) and ultimately to the Land and Environment Court (Mark- och miljödomstolen).
