Lägenhetsregistret

Lägenhetsregistret is Sweden's national apartment register maintained by Statistics Sweden (SCB), recording every residential dwelling's unique identifier, address, area, and characteristics — a key reference for property due diligence.

Sweden's lägenhetsregistret (apartment register) was established by the Act on the Register of Dwellings (lag om lägenhetsregister) and is maintained by Statistics Sweden (Statistiska centralbyrån, SCB). Every residential dwelling — whether a house, apartment, or other dwelling — is assigned a unique lägenhets-ID (dwelling identifier).

The register records the dwelling's address, area (in square metres), number of rooms, building year, ownership form (hyresrätt, bostadsrätt, or äganderätt), and heating type. Local authorities and housing organisations use this data for planning, and lenders reference it for mortgage assessments.

Buyers of bostadsrätter (tenant-owned apartments) can cross-reference the registered area in lägenhetsregistret with the area stated in the association's register. Discrepancies can affect valuation and mortgage calculations.

The register is also used to verify the official address of a dwelling, which has implications for population registration (folkbokföring). A buyer moving to Sweden must register at the dwelling's address per the registered data.

Access to the register is available through Lantmäteriet's property information services and through SCB. Estate agents and banks routinely query it during due diligence. Buyers can request a utdrag (extract) to confirm registered characteristics match the listing.

⚠ Common MistakeBuyers sometimes assume the area stated in a listing is the same as the registered area. Listing areas can include balconies or non-heated spaces. The registered area in lägenhetsregistret is the standardised basis for valuations.
💡 Expert TipAsk your estate agent to pull the lägenhetsregistret extract for any apartment you are seriously considering. Compare the registered area and room count with the listing. Any discrepancy should be investigated before making an offer.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the lägenhetsregister the same as the fastighetsregister?

No. The fastighetsregister records land and building units; the lägenhetsregister records individual dwellings within buildings. Both are maintained by Swedish authorities and complement each other.

Can I access lägenhetsregistret data myself?

Yes, through Lantmäteriet's e-services (lantmateriet.se) or through registered data services. Some data is freely available; detailed extracts may require a fee.

Does lägenhetsregistret cover all types of housing?

It covers all permanent residential dwellings. Holiday homes and secondary residences are included if they have permanent addresses. Commercial premises are not covered.
