Fastighetsmäklare

Fastighetsmäklare Sweden – Estate Agent Rules and Commission

A fastighetsmäklare (real estate agent) in Sweden is a state-licensed professional regulated by the Swedish Estate Agents Inspectorate (Fastighetsmäklarinspektionen, FMI). Unlike in many countries, Swedish estate agents are legally required to act as neutral intermediaries — they represent neither seller nor buyer exclusively but owe duties to both parties.

Licensing requires a university-level education (fastighetsmäklarutbildning of 120 credits), practical experience, and passing an examination. All licensed agents must be registered with the FMI. Using an unlicensed person to transact Swedish property is illegal. Buyers can verify any agent's registration at fmi.se.

The agent's commission (provision) is typically 1.5–3% of the sale price plus VAT, though it varies by location and market. Commission is paid by the seller, not the buyer. Agents are prohibited from receiving undisclosed payments from both buyer and seller simultaneously — the neutrality obligation prevents double-commission arrangements.

The agent prepares the purchase contract (köpekontraktet) and ensures both parties understand its terms. They are also required to disclose to buyers all information about the property that may be material to the purchase decision — including any known defects flagged by the seller. Failure to disclose can give rise to liability.

Disputes with fastighetsmäklare are handled by the Fastighetsmäklarinspektionen (disciplinary matters) and, for financial claims, by the general courts. Agents must carry professional indemnity insurance (ansvarsförsäkring) of at least SEK 2 million per claim.

⚠ Common MistakeAssuming the Swedish estate agent represents only the seller's interests. The agent's neutrality duty means they are required to disclose material information to you as a buyer — use this to your advantage during due diligence.
💡 Expert TipReview the hemnet.se listing information carefully before viewing — Swedish agents are required to upload energy performance certificates and housing association accounts for bostadsrätter. Reviewing these documents before the viewing saves time and reveals issues early.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can an estate agent refuse to represent me as a buyer?

Swedish agents represent the transaction rather than individual clients. A buyer does not formally instruct an agent — the seller instructs the agent for the listing, but the agent owes neutrality duties to all parties.

What can I do if the agent withheld information that affected my purchase?

File a complaint with FMI for disciplinary proceedings and/or bring a damages claim in court. The professional indemnity insurance provides the financial backstop for valid claims.

Is there a fixed commission rate in Sweden?

No. Commission rates are freely negotiable between seller and agent. In competitive seller markets, sellers have less leverage to negotiate down; in slower markets, rates can be discussed more freely.
