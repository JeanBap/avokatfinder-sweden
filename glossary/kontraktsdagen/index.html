Kontraktsdagen

Kontraktsdagen Sweden – Contract Day in Property Transactions

In Swedish property transactions, kontraktsdagen (contract day) is the occasion when both buyer and seller sign the legally binding purchase agreement (köpekontraktet). It typically takes place within a few days of the auction (budgivning) or price agreement being reached, before the completion date (tillträde) several weeks later.

The köpekontraktet signed on kontraktsdagen sets out all legally binding terms: parties, property description, purchase price, handpenning amount, tillträdesdagen (completion date), any conditions (such as financing clauses), and the state and condition of the property. Once signed, both parties are legally bound — withdrawal by either party triggers the forfeiture or return of the handpenning and potential damages.

The estate agent prepares and conducts the signing meeting. Both buyer and seller must be present in person or via a power of attorney. The buyer pays the handpenning (usually 10%) on kontraktsdagen — either by bank transfer in advance or by certified means on the day. The agent holds the handpenning on behalf of the seller.

Key Facts

OccasionSigning of köpekontraktet (purchase agreement)
DepositHandpenning (10%) paid on this date
Binding effectImmediate — withdrawal triggers financial consequences
Agent rolePrepares contract, conducts signing
ConditionsVillkor must be exercised/waived by specified deadlines

For new-build purchases directly from developers, a förhandsavtal (advance contract) may be used before the building is completed, followed by the final köpekontraktet when possession can be given. Förhandsavtal are specifically regulated under the Act on Housing (bostadsköpslagen) and provide specific cancellation rights.

If any conditions (villkor) are included in the contract — such as a financing clause or a subject-to-survey condition — they must be satisfied or waived by the deadline specified in the contract. Failure to meet the deadline for exercising or waiving a condition can have significant legal consequences for both parties.

⚠ Common MistakeAssuming conditions (villkor) protect you indefinitely. Each condition has a specific deadline — missing the deadline to exercise or waive a condition can bind you to the contract unconditionally.
💡 Expert TipReview the entire köpekontraktet 24 hours before kontraktsdagen. The agent must provide it in advance. Signing without reading risks agreeing to unfavourable conditions; in Sweden's competitive market, buyers often feel pressure to sign quickly — resist this for consequential terms.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can kontraktsdagen be conducted remotely?

Yes. Electronic signatures (BankID) are accepted for köpekontraktet in Sweden. Remote signings became common during COVID-19 and remain available.

What if I want to pull out after kontraktsdagen?

You can withdraw, but you will forfeit the handpenning and may owe additional damages if the seller suffers a loss exceeding the handpenning amount. Legal advice before withdrawing is essential.

Is there a cooling-off period in Sweden?

No. For private property sales, there is no statutory cooling-off right after signing. The contract is binding from the moment of signature.
