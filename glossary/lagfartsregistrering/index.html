Lagfart

Lagfart Sweden – Title Registration in the Land Register

Lagfart is the official registration of property ownership in the Swedish land register (fastighetsregistret), maintained by the National Land Survey (Lantmäteriet). It must be applied for within three months of the acquisition (köpekontraktet) and is the definitive record of who owns Swedish real property.

The lagfart application is submitted to Lantmäteriet and must include the original deed of sale (fångeshandling — the signed köpekontraktet), proof of payment of stämpelskatt (stamp duty), and the applicant's personal details. Lantmäteriet processes the application and updates the fastighetsregistret.

Stämpelskatt (stamp duty) is payable on lagfart registration at a rate of 1.5% of the purchase price (or the tax assessment value if higher) for private individuals, and 4.25% for legal entities (companies). This is one of the main transaction costs in Swedish real property purchases. There are limited exemptions, notably for certain corporate restructurings.

If lagfart is not applied for within three months, Lantmäteriet can impose a penalty fee (förseningsavgift). More significantly, an unregistered buyer may face difficulties if the seller subsequently transfers the same property to a different buyer who then registers — Swedish land register priority rules favour the first registered buyer.

For bostadsrätter, lagfart does not apply — there is no land register entry for cooperative rights. Ownership of a bostadsrätt is recorded in the BRF's own share register (lägenhetsförteckning). The distinction between real property (where lagfart applies) and bostadsrätt (where it does not) is fundamental in Swedish property law.

⚠ Common MistakeDelaying the lagfart application beyond three months. Penalty fees accrue daily, and unregistered title creates priority risk if the seller is fraudulent or becomes insolvent.
💡 Expert TipSubmit the lagfart application the same week as completion. Your estate agent can facilitate the submission but is not legally required to. If using a lawyer, confirm in advance who is responsible for the application.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can lagfart be applied for before completion?

No. The application requires the signed deed, which is only available after completion. However, preparation can begin immediately on kontraktsdagen.

What if Lantmäteriet rejects the lagfart application?

Rejections are usually due to missing documents or incorrect stamp duty payment. The deficiency must be corrected within the time Lantmäteriet specifies or the application lapses.

Does lagfart guarantee title?

Registration creates a strong presumption of title but does not provide the same absolute State guarantee as some other European land register systems. However, a registered owner in good faith is generally protected against prior unregistered claims.
