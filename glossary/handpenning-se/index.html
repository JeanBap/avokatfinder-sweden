Handpenning

Handpenning Sweden – Property Purchase Deposit Explained

The handpenning is the deposit paid by the buyer when the purchase agreement (köpekontraktet) is signed in Sweden. It is typically 10% of the purchase price and is paid to the seller on contract day (kontraktsdagen), with the balance due on the completion date (tillträdesdagen) weeks later.

Unlike some countries, Sweden does not require the handpenning to be held in a neutral escrow account by default. It is typically paid directly to the seller (or held by the estate agent on behalf of the seller). If the sale does not complete due to the buyer's default, the seller can retain the handpenning as liquidated damages.

If the sale fails to complete due to the seller's default, the buyer is entitled to the return of the handpenning plus potentially additional damages. However, recovery in practice may require legal action. Buyers should ensure the contract clearly states the conditions under which the handpenning is refundable.

Key Facts

Standard amount10% of purchase price
Payment timingOn contract signing (kontraktsdagen)
Default by buyerSeller retains handpenning
Default by sellerBuyer entitled to return + damages
Financing conditionFinansieringsvillkor — protects buyer if mortgage refused

Swedish property purchases involving mortgage financing commonly include a financing condition (finansieringsvillkor) that allows the buyer to withdraw and recover the handpenning if their mortgage application is refused. The terms and deadline for exercising this condition must be precisely drafted in the contract.

The handpenning is paid on the day the köpekontraktet is signed — not on the day of possession. Buyers must ensure their funds are available before contract signing, not just on completion day. Failure to pay the handpenning when due is itself a breach entitling the seller to cancel.

⚠ Common MistakeNot including a clear finansieringsvillkor when buying subject to mortgage approval. Without this clause, if your mortgage is refused, you may lose the handpenning.
💡 Expert TipNegotiate the handpenning amount and refund conditions carefully before contract day. In a buyers' market, 5% deposits are sometimes accepted; in competitive markets, sellers may push for more. Always have the financing condition deadline reviewed by a lawyer.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the handpenning the same as a reservation deposit?

In Swedish practice, there is typically no pre-contract reservation deposit. The handpenning is paid at formal contract signing. Some agents use a letter of intent (avsiktsförklaring) before this, but it carries no legal obligation.

What happens if I cannot pay the handpenning on contract day?

Non-payment is a breach of contract. The seller may terminate the agreement and claim damages. Ensure your funds are cleared and available on the agreed date.

Can the handpenning be less than 10%?

Yes, the amount is freely negotiable. For investment properties or when buyer and seller agree, any amount can be set in the contract.
