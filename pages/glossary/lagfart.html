Lagfart

Definition: Swedish legal document that formally transfers property ownership from one party to another.

Overview

Lagfart is the Swedish legal instrument used to formally transfer ownership of real property. It must be registered with the Swedish Land Registry (Lantmäteriet) to be valid and binding.

In a lagfart transaction, the seller (försäljare) transfers their ownership rights to the buyer (köpare). The document must be signed by both parties and include essential information about the property and transaction.

The lagfart requires a legitimate transfer fee (lagfartavgift) based on the property's purchase price. This fee is paid to the Swedish government as part of the registration process.

Once registered, the lagfart is the official evidence of ownership. It replaces any previous owner's claims and provides the new owner with legal title and the right to use, modify, and dispose of the property.

Swedish law requires that lagfart be registered within certain time frames. Failure to register promptly can result in disputes about who actually owns the property or create complications in later transactions.

Key Facts

  • Transfers legal ownership of Swedish real property
  • Must be signed by both seller and buyer
  • Subject to lagfartavgift (registration fee)
  • Registered with Swedish Land Registry (Lantmäteriet)
  • Provides official proof of ownership

Common Mistake

Watch out: Delaying the lagfart registration after purchase, which leaves the new owner's legal title incomplete and can complicate financing or future sales.

Pro Tip

💡 Always use a Swedish lawyer to prepare the lagfart and ensure all terms are correct before signing, as errors can be costly to fix.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the lagfartavgift and who pays it?

The lagfartavgift is a registration fee (typically 1.5% of the purchase price) paid by the buyer to transfer ownership.

How long after purchase must I register the lagfart?

It should be registered as soon as possible, but legally within a few months. Delays can complicate your ownership rights.

Can I transfer property without lagfart?

No. The lagfart registration is essential to legally transfer and prove ownership of Swedish real property.
