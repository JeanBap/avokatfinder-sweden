Fastighetsregistrering

Definition: Swedish property registration system that records ownership and encumbrances on real estate.

Overview

Fastighetsregistrering is Sweden's official system for registering real property ownership and any rights or encumbrances on that property. Maintained by the Swedish Land Registry (Lantmäteriet), it provides a public record of property titles.

Every property in Sweden receives a unique identifier (fastighhetsbeteckning) that remains constant even if ownership changes. This system ensures clarity about who owns what land.

The registration includes information about mortgages, liens, easements, and other legal interests in the property. This protects both owners and creditors by providing a transparent record.

When property changes hands, the fastighetsregistrering must be updated through a formal transfer process called lagfart. This ensures the new owner is officially recognized as the legal proprietor.

Fastighetsregistrering is accessible to the public, allowing anyone to verify property ownership, check for encumbrances, and determine if a property is subject to mortgages or other obligations.

Key Facts

  • Maintained by Swedish Land Registry (Lantmäteriet)
  • Each property has a unique identifier (fastighhetsbeteckning)
  • Records ownership, mortgages, liens, and easements
  • Provides public access to property information
  • Updated through lagfart when ownership transfers

Common Mistake

Watch out: Assuming that a property is free of encumbrances without checking the fastighetsregister; always verify before purchasing.

Pro Tip

💡 Request a full extract (registerutdrag) from Lantmäteriet before buying property to see all recorded interests and obligations.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I find information about a specific property in the fastighetsregistrering?

Visit Lantmäteriet's website or office with the property address or fastighhetsbeteckning to request a registerutdrag (property extract).

What information is public in the fastighetsregistrering?

Ownership, mortgages, liens, easements, and other registered encumbrances are generally public, but some details may be restricted.

How long does it take to register a property transfer?

Typically 2-4 weeks after the lagfart application is submitted, depending on Lantmäteriet's workload.
