Inteckning

Definition: A Swedish mortgage lien registered against property to secure a loan.

Overview

Inteckning is a Swedish legal mechanism by which a property is mortgaged or pledged as security for a debt, typically a bank loan. The lender registers the inteckning in the property register to secure their claim.

When a property owner borrows money from a bank or other lender, the lender often requires an inteckning as collateral. This gives the lender the right to seize and sell the property if the borrower defaults on the loan.

The inteckning is registered in the fastighetsregistrering (property register) and is visible to all interested parties. Multiple intecknings can be registered on the same property, with priority determined by registration order.

In case of borrower default, the lender can initiate foreclosure proceedings (exekution) to sell the property. The proceeds are used to pay the secured debt, with any remainder going to the property owner.

Swedish law strictly regulates intecknings to protect borrowers and ensure fair lending practices. The amount of an inteckning cannot exceed the property's assessed value, and lenders have specific obligations regarding disclosure and documentation.

Key Facts

  • Registered as security for a loan against property
  • Appears in the property register (fastighetsregistrering)
  • Multiple intecknings can be registered on same property
  • Lender can foreclose if borrower defaults
  • Amount limited to property value

Common Mistake

Watch out: Not understanding that intecknings reduce your equity and that foreclosure is possible if you miss payments.

Pro Tip

💡 Review the inteckning terms carefully and ensure you understand the interest rate, payment schedule, and conditions for foreclosure.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between inteckning and a regular loan?

An inteckning is a mortgage lien; the loan is the debt itself. The inteckning secures the loan with the property.

Can I remove an inteckning from my property?

Yes, once the secured debt is paid off, you can request the lender remove the inteckning from the register.

What happens if I sell property with an inteckning?

The sale proceeds are used to pay off the inteckning debt, with any remainder going to the seller.
