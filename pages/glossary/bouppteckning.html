Bouppteckning

Definition: A Swedish estate inventory document listing all property and debts left by a deceased person.

Overview

Bouppteckning is a formal Swedish legal document that inventories all assets and liabilities of a deceased person's estate. It must be prepared within three months of death and signed by the executor and all heirs.

This document serves as the official record for estate administration, detailing real property, personal property, financial accounts, and outstanding debts. Banks and creditors require a copy before releasing funds or settling claims.

The bouppteckning is essential for determining if the estate is solvent and for calculating inheritance taxes. It provides transparency among heirs about the true value of their inheritance.

In Swedish law, preparing a thorough bouppteckning protects both the executor from liability and the heirs from disputes. All parties must sign and approve the inventory.

The document remains on file with the Swedish enforcement authority (kronofogdemyndigheten) and can be requested by interested parties, making it a public record of the estate's composition.

Key Facts

  • Must be completed within 3 months of death
  • Requires signatures from executor and all adult heirs
  • Lists all assets including real property, vehicles, and bank accounts
  • Documents all debts and liabilities against the estate
  • Filed with Swedish enforcement authority for public record

Common Mistake

Watch out: Undervaluing assets or forgetting to list bank accounts and insurance policies, which can lead to disputes among heirs.

Pro Tip

💡 Keep detailed receipts and valuations. Items like antiques or heirlooms may require professional appraisal for accurate inventory.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who is responsible for preparing the bouppteckning?

The executor (testamentexekutor) or estate administrator (dödsboförvaltare) is responsible for preparing it with input from all heirs.

Can the bouppteckning be challenged by heirs?

Yes, if heirs believe the inventory is incomplete or inaccurate, they can request amendments before final approval.

What happens if there are unpaid debts listed?

The estate pays debts from its assets before distributing inheritances. Creditors must file claims within a specified period.
